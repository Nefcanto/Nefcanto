Brown Eyes

"A man is pretending to be me?" I said. "Why?"

Every year, Peter and Sudan Reed go to Lea-on-Sea for their holiday; every year they stay at the Hotel Vista.

This year things start to go wrong. A man there is pretending to be Peter. But why? Is he friendly or dangerous? What does he want?

Will this, their thirteenth visit to Lean-on-Sea, be their last visit?

Pual Stewart lives with his family in Bridhton, and writes books for children and young people. He has one son - his name is Joseph, and one daughter - her name is Anna.

He was a teacher of English in Greece, Germany and Sri Lanka. He went to Kenya, India, Australia, Malaysia, America and all over Europe, too. But never to Lean-on-Sea!

We arrived at our hotel in Lea-on-Sea early on Saturday morning. The Hotel Vista. Susan and I always stay there. It is very quet, very friendly and the food is good. I opened the door, and we walked in.

"Good morning, Mrs Brown" I said.

"Mr and Mrs Reed" she said. "It's good to see you again".

"It's ood to be here again." I said.

"Cup of coffee?" she said. "Before you go up to your room".

"Thank you" I said.

"Where's little Mary?" asked Susan. Mary was Mrs Brown's daughter.

"She's in the garden" said Mrs Brown and laughed.

"But she's not little! Mary's a tall young woman now".

"How old is she?" Susan asked.

"Fifteen". said Mrs Brown.

"Fifteen!" I said. "Time goes quickly!".

"I know" said Mrs Brown. "Now, coffee! Before I forget again" she walked to the kitchen.

Susan looked at me. "I love it here, Peter" she said.

"I know" I said.

"When did we first meet?" she asked. "Eleven years ago? Tweleve?"

"Wrong" I said. "It'll be thirteen years on Tuesday" I said. "In the cafe".

"Ah, yes" said Susan, and shut her eyes.

It was our fourth night at the Hotel Vista. We were at a table in the restaurant, but I did not want to eat. I was afraid. Very afraid. Things were not right in Lea-on-Sea.

On Sunday, it was the old man in the newspaper shop.

On Monday, the main in the bank, and the woman in the cinema.

Yesterday, the girl in the she shop.

And this afternoon, the woman in the Italian restaurant.

All of them smiled at me and said, "Hello again!".

"A man is pretending to be me," I said. "Why?"

"I don't know", said Susan. "But it's not important We're ..."

"Not important?" I shouted. "I think it is. I ... I'm going to the police!"

"No" said Susan. "They'll laugh at you. We'll find the main. Lean-on-Sea isn't very big. It'll be easy."

I looked down. I didn't want to meet the man!

Susan looked into my eyes. She took my hand. "I'm afraid, too." she said.

Later that evening, we walked down to the sea. The sun was red and yellow. The water was light blue.

"Today is an important day" said Susan.

"Important?" I said.

"Thirteen years" she said. "You and me! Did you forget?"

"I? ... Yes, I forgot". I said quietly.

"Do you love me?" Susand asked.

"Oh, yes" I said, and turned to her.

"Good" she said. "I loved you too".

We kissed, and for the first time on our holiday, I was happy!.

Suddenly Susan moved back.

"Look!" she was right. "it's him! At the cafe!"

She was riht. There was a man with a big nose and black hair. He shut the cafe door and turned right. At the cinema, he turned right again, and walked quickly away.

"Run!" said Susan. "We don't want to lose him".

We arrived at the cinema and looked down the road.

"Where is he?" I said.

"There!" said Susan.

I saw him turn left at the bank.

"Quickly!" I shouted.

We ran across the road after the man.

"Don't go!" I shouted.

But he didn't hear me. We ran to the bank. There, we stopped. I looked up and down the road.

The man was not there.

"Where is he?" I said.

"I don't knkow" said Susan. "But we'll see him aain. I know we will. Come on." she said. "We'll have a drink at the hotel".

We walked back to the Hotel Vista slowly. Susan looked in the cafes and restaurants but she didn't see the man again. Luckily!

"Perhaps we'll never see him again" I said.

"Perhaps" said Susan guietly.

We arrived at the door of the hotel at ten o'clock.

We heard the television. We walked in. We saw Mrs Brown, but she didn't see us.

I smiled. "Sleeping" I said.
